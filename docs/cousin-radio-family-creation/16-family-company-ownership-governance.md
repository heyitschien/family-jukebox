# 16 — Family Company Ownership and Governance Model

This document defines the recommended operating structure for Cousin Radio as a founder-led, family-inspired company.

This is a planning document, not legal advice. Before issuing equity, forming an entity, hiring family members, or taking payment, review the structure with a qualified attorney and tax professional.

## Recommended Model

```text
Founder-led, family-inspired, professionally operated.
```

Cousin Radio should begin as a clear founder-led company, with Chien as the primary owner, CEO, chief product leader, and engineering lead.

The family can participate as advisors, storytellers, testers, ambassadors, creative collaborators, and cultural guardians without requiring early equal ownership or unclear business authority.

## Why This Model

Cousin Radio needs two things at the same time:

1. A strong creative and technical center.
2. A warm family culture that gives the product its soul.

Equal family ownership too early can create confusion around authority, contribution, compensation, decision-making, and family relationships.

Solo-only ownership can move quickly, but may miss the deeper family wisdom, testing, and emotional grounding that make Cousin Radio special.

The recommended middle path is:

```text
Chien leads the company.
Family helps shape the culture, use cases, stories, testing, and emotional quality.
```

## Product Direction

```text
Cousin Radio turns love, jokes, memories, and tiny everyday moments into songs worth sharing.
```

## North Star

```text
Songs for the people we love.
```

## Founder Role

### Chien — Founder / CEO / Product / Engineering

Chien owns final decision authority for:

- product direction
- roadmap
- technical architecture
- design direction
- hiring and contractor decisions
- budget decisions
- legal and provider decisions
- release timing
- brand standards
- repository and deployment control
- investor or partnership conversations

This keeps the company moving quickly and avoids family ambiguity.

## Family Participation Model

Family participation should be real, respected, and structured.

Potential family roles:

### Creative Director / Visual Story Advisor

Possible fit: Eve, if she wants the role.

Focus:

- photography
- visual identity
- album cover taste
- family storytelling
- memory presentation
- emotional quality of the app

### Family Experience Advisor

Possible fit: mother-in-law.

Focus:

- grandparent usability
- emotional resonance
- cultural warmth
- family traditions
- whether the app feels natural to non-technical users

### Community and Beta Testing Circle

Possible fit: sisters-in-law, cousins, nieces, nephews, friends.

Focus:

- testing song creation flows
- sharing feedback
- identifying awkward moments
- suggesting family use cases
- helping validate whether people would actually send songs

### Family Historian / Story Contributor

Possible fit: father-in-law, elders, parents, aunties, uncles.

Focus:

- family memories
- recipes
- faith and cultural stories
- elder perspectives
- wisdom archives

## Family Council

Create an informal Cousin Radio Family Council.

Recommended cadence:

```text
Monthly during active exploration
Quarterly once the product stabilizes
```

Purpose:

- review product direction
- listen to new songs
- discuss family feedback
- identify use cases
- test whether the app feels simple and beautiful
- protect the heart of the product

The Family Council advises. Chien decides.

## Decision Rights

Use this decision model:

```text
Input: family, users, advisors, beta testers
Decision: Chien
Execution: Chien and approved collaborators
Accountability: Chien
```

This avoids confusion while still honoring the family contribution.

## Ownership Recommendation

Early stage recommendation:

```text
Keep ownership simple and founder-controlled.
```

Do not divide equity casually during the idea stage.

Reasons:

- contribution levels will vary
- business risk is not equal for everyone
- legal and tax complexity increases
- family relationships can be strained by unclear expectations
- decision-making can slow down

If the business becomes real and revenue-generating, family compensation or equity can be considered later through clear written agreements.

## Contribution vs Ownership

Not every contribution should become ownership.

Possible contribution categories:

```text
Idea feedback
Creative input
Beta testing
Song/story contribution
Part-time project help
Paid contractor work
Formal executive work
Capital investment
```

Each category should be treated differently.

For example:

- feedback may be acknowledged
- song/story contributions may be credited
- part-time project work may be paid
- formal executive work may require compensation or equity discussion
- capital investment requires legal documentation

## Compensation Possibilities Later

If the project grows, possible structures include:

- paid contractor agreements
- advisor agreements
- revenue share for specific creative assets
- formal employment
- equity grants with vesting
- profit-sharing after profitability

Do not promise compensation before the business model is clear.

## Family Relationship Protection

Cousin Radio should strengthen family, not create conflict.

Guardrails:

- no vague equity promises
- no pressure to work for free
- no hidden expectations
- no family member gets authority without a defined role
- no public sharing of family material without consent
- business disagreement should not become family rejection

## Recommended Initial Language

When explaining the structure to family:

```text
I want Cousin Radio to be family-inspired and family-supported, but founder-led so it stays clear and executable. I will hold product and business decision authority while inviting family feedback, testing, stories, and creative guidance. If the company later generates revenue or needs formal roles, we can discuss fair compensation or written agreements at that stage.
```

## What To Avoid

Avoid saying:

```text
Everyone owns this equally.
```

Avoid promising:

```text
We will all get rich from this.
```

Avoid unclear statements like:

```text
Just help now and we will figure it out later.
```

Better:

```text
Let us explore this together with clear roles, clear expectations, and respect for family relationships.
```

## Final Governance Principle

Cousin Radio can be a family heirloom and a real company only if the family love and the business structure are both protected.

The recommended structure is:

```text
Chien leads.
Family inspires.
The product serves the people we love.
```
