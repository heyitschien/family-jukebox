# Cousin Radio — Living Family Culture System

> Directional note for the radio.
>
> This document captures the long-term product, family, and cultural vision behind Cousin Radio.

---

# Core Direction

Cousin Radio should not only be treated as a music app.

It is becoming:

> **a living family culture system**

A place where the family can continuously create, preserve, organize, and replay its own music, memories, celebrations, jokes, stories, and traditions.

This matters because the family is not only consuming culture.

The family is creating its own culture.

---

# The Scale Opportunity

If the family has around 20 people, the catalog can grow quickly.

Rough math:

- **20 people × 1 song/month** = **240 songs/year**
- **20 people × 1 song/week** = **1,040 songs/year**
- **20 people × 1 song/day** = **7,300 songs/year**

The point is not only quantity.

The real value is variety, memory, and participation.

Each person brings different stories, moods, tastes, jokes, seasons, and relationships.

# Long-Term Vision

Cousin Radio can become something very few families in history have had:

> **an intentional, multigenerational cultural archive**

A place where the family can hear itself across time.

Not just photos.
Not just videos.
Not just text messages.

Songs.

Music carries emotion differently.

A song can bring someone back to a season of life instantly.

Twenty years from now, a child may hear a song from Cousin Radio and remember:

- who they were
- who loved them
- what the family felt like
- where we went
- what we laughed about
- what we celebrated

That is legacy.

# Contribution Philosophy

People do not need to be musicians to participate.

Grandma can say:

> I want a song about making bánh chưng with the grandchildren.

A parent can say:

> I want a birthday song for my daughter.

A child can say:

> I want a song about sharks, basketball, and space.

Someone can bring:

- one sentence
- one memory
- one photo
- one joke
- one family phrase
- one feeling

The system can help turn that into music.

The family provides the heart.

AI and software provide the amplification.

# Product Implications

As the catalog grows, Cousin Radio will need systems for organization, discovery, and family participation.

Potential future features:

## 1. Family Contributor Profiles

Each person can have a profile:

- songs about them
- songs they requested
- songs they helped create
- favorite playlists
- family role

## 2. Song Request Flow

A simple family-friendly form:

- request a song
- choose mood
- choose style
- add memory
- upload photo/reference
- mark private/public

## 3. Approval Workflow

For family trust:

- draft song
- family review
- approve before publishing
- protect kids and sensitive memories

## 4. Collections

Organize songs by:

- person
- event
- holiday
- year
- mood
- location
- family branch
- private/public

## 5. Family Radio Modes

Listening modes:

- Family Mix
- Kids Mix
- Road Trip Mode
- Christmas Mode
- Birthday Mode
- Grandparents Mix
- New Songs
- Memory Lane

## 6. Archive Metadata

Every song should preserve:

- date created
- who requested it
- who it is about
- source memory
- event / holiday
- privacy level
- lyrics
- prompt
- artwork
- notes

# Operating Principle

Cousin Radio should make family creativity easy, joyful, and safe.

The product should always ask:

- Does this help the family create?
- Does this help the family remember?
- Does this help the family feel seen?
- Does this protect privacy and dignity?
- Does this help future generations understand who we were?

---

# Important Framing

In the age of abundant intelligence, families can become their own:

- studios
- publishers
- archives
- storytellers
- cultural institutions
- memory keepers

This does not mean everything needs to become public.

Some of the best culture is private.

Some songs are only for the family.

Some memories should stay inside the family circle.

Cousin Radio should support both:

- private family memory
- shareable public celebration
